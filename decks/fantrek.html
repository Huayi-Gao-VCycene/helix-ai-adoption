The Fan's Problem

The Demand Is Emotional.
The Logistics Are a Mess.

No Crew
Many fans don't have friends who follow the same teams. A ticket alone doesn't create the experience — the people around you do. Right now, there's no organized way to find them.
DIY Friction
Fans who want away-game weekends must self-assemble everything: tickets, hotels, transport, pre-game plans. First-timers hesitate. Veterans get burned out re-planning the same trip alone.
Safety Gap
Safety and belonging are real considerations for women traveling to unfamiliar cities for games. FanTrek packages both into one curated experience.

Market Size

A Niche With Unusually Strong
Spend and Identity.

Sports Tourism
Sports tourism is one of the fastest-growing travel segments. Fans spend on experiences differently than general tourists — they book around emotion, identity, and belonging, not price comparison.
Women's Fandom
Women's sports fandom is community-driven. These fans organize, they advocate, they bring others in. The core fan doesn't just buy a ticket — they recruit four friends to come.
Pull Effect
Core fans pull casual fans into the experience. FanTrek's strongest conversion mechanism is the existing community — one die-hard fan's post-trip story converts three people who weren't sure they'd come.
